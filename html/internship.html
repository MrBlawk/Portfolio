Augmented Reality

Augmented Reality Business Card

GGZ Friesland wished for a modern, even futuristic, business card. The idea of creating an AR product had been floating around the Innovation department for a while, and since I had some experience I picked up the project. My idea was to use two libraries; Three.js and AR.js to create a fully functional AR prototype with clickable links and playable videos hovering on the screen of your phone. Another advantage is the reduction of the carbon footprint of using paper or even plastic business cards at conventions. To make it fully functional I made a QR code that would redirect your phone to the webpage running the AR code, prompting you to let it use your camera. When the camera gets activated, a marker for tracking is in the middle of the QR. This way I could use one image to redirect people to my code and immediately have them run the AR business card on their phones.

Augmented Reality

Development halted after second test

Unfortunately I found out that the libraries that are commonly used for similar web apps are not yet well suited for mobile use. This caused some rather odd bugs with the display not viewing or passing the correct aspect ratio. Since the origin was rooted deeply in the rendering API used in Three.js, I halted further development to shift my focus back to the Virtual Reality experience. I did learn a lot about web apps for mobile use during this project, and am always willing to explain my code of this AR prototype. I can't share it publicly due to it being owned by GGZ Friesland.

Brainstorm Toolkit (yes, it's me in the back of the picture)

Developing the Brainstormtoolkit

The Innovation department of GGZ Friesland is often asked to help other internal teams with brainstorming sessions. This is usually for subjects such as coming up with a new mission and vision, but also for problem solving and improving certain processes. To help out the innovation team, I have been working on a Brainstorm Toolkit, that would help these teams to energize, diverge and converge in their own brainstorming sessions. This toolkit has a guide that makes it possible for the teams to be completely independent during these sessions, requiring no help or explanations from the Innovation department.
